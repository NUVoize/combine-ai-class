# Lesson 3.5: Carrying Your Character into Video

This is the core move of the whole video tier, the direct parallel to image-to-image in the cloud path (Lesson 1.6) and the LoRA in the local path (Lesson 2.8). The question is the same one that has driven the entire course: how do I get the *actual* visual information for my character in, instead of describing it? In video, the strongest answer is to start from a still you already locked, and animate that.

## The main path: image-to-video from a locked still

Here is the move, and it ties everything together:

1. **Generate a perfect still of your character**, ideally with the LoRA you trained in Level 2, in the pose and framing you want the shot to start from.
2. **Feed that still into an image-to-video model** (Lesson 3.2) as the starting frame.
3. **The clip animates from a correct identity**, because frame one is genuinely your character, not a guess.

Starting from a right frame is the single biggest thing you can do for video consistency. The model is no longer inventing your character from words, it is moving a character it was handed.

## Other ways to condition

Depending on the tool, you may also have:

- **Character or reference conditioning** built into the video model, where you supply reference images of your character directly.
- **Video-to-video** driven by a real reference clip for motion, with your character's look applied on top (Lesson 3.2).

## The limits, honestly

This carries identity *in*, but it does not lock it forever, the same honest caveat from the still world:

- The face can still drift as the clip plays, especially over longer clips (Lesson 3.4).
- Big or fast motion drifts more than calm motion.
- The start frame anchors the beginning, but the model still has freedom as it moves away from it.

So you manage video drift, you do not eliminate it, which is why short clips and deliberate planning matter so much.
