# Lesson 2.8: What a LoRA Is and Why It Works

This is the concept the whole course has been building toward. Since Lesson 0.5 the problem has been the same: text cannot carry a face, so you have to feed the visual information in some other way. The cloud path fed it in one image at a time, from the outside. A **LoRA** is how you feed it in permanently, from the inside, by teaching the model your character so it carries the identity itself. This is the real lock.

Let's understand it before we train one.

## The plain-English version

A big image model already knows an enormous amount about the visual world. You do not want to rebuild it. You just want to add one thing: your character. A LoRA is a small add-on that you train on your dataset and then attach to the base model. With it attached, the model can now produce *your* character on demand. Detach it, and the model is back to normal. Think of it as a small expansion that teaches the base model one new person without disturbing the rest of what it knows.

That "small and attachable" quality is the whole point. It is why LoRAs are practical to train on modest hardware and easy to share, unlike retraining a whole model.

> **[FILL LATER: simple diagram]** Base model plus LoRA equals your character on tap. One clean visual of the add-on attaching to the base.

## Why it solves the consistency problem for good

Recall the cloud ceiling (Lesson 1.9): you were always steering from the outside, and nothing was ever locked. A LoRA changes the location of the knowledge. The character is no longer something you describe or hand in as a reference each time. It is now *inside the model*, baked into how it generates. Ask for your character in any scene, any pose, and the model produces them because it genuinely knows them now.

This is the move from Lesson 0.5 in its strongest form: not describing the face, not showing one reference, but teaching the model the character until it carries the visual information on its own.

> **[FILL LATER: the payoff demo]** Your trained character generated across wildly different scenes, poses, and styles, holding identity throughout. The result the entire course has been promising, finally delivered.

## What a LoRA is not

A LoRA is not magic and not a full retrain. It is a focused nudge to a model that already knows almost everything. That focus is its strength, and, as the next lessons show, also the source of its quirks. Teaching a model something new always changes it a little, and understanding that trade is what separates people who train one good LoRA from people who train a thousand.
