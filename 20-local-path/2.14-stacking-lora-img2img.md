# Lesson 2.14: Stacking LoRA and Image-to-Image Together

This is where the local path comes together into one move. You have a LoRA that locks **who** (Module 2C) and image-to-image that locks the **starting composition** (Lesson 2.13). Stack them and you get the thing the whole course has been driving at: your exact character, in the exact pose and layout you want, on demand. This lesson is about running both controls at once and balancing them.

## The combined workflow

The pattern is simple once you see it:

1. **Start from a reference image** that has the pose, composition, or structure you want. It does not need to be your character at all. It is there for its *layout*.
2. **Attach your character LoRA**, so the model knows who to render.
3. **Run image-to-image** from that reference. The LoRA supplies the identity, the source image supplies the arrangement.
4. **Out comes your character**, in that pose, in that composition.

You are feeding the model two streams of control at once: identity from the LoRA, structure from the image. Neither could do this alone.

## A note on dedicated control tools

Image-to-image carries structure loosely. For tighter control over pose and layout, there is a family of dedicated **control** tools (the ControlNet world) that guide a generation from a structural reference. The input you feed them, the pose map, the edge outline, the depth guide, is properly called a **control set** or **reference dataset**, which is the real, technical meaning of that term (distinct from the *training* dataset you built in Module 2B). When you want a pose hit exactly, this is the direction to grow into.

## Balancing the controls

The craft is balance. You now have several strengths in play at once: the LoRA's strength, the image-to-image strength, and any control strength. Crank them all and they fight, exactly the strain from Lessons 2.11 and 2.12. The skill is finding the mix where identity holds, composition holds, and the model still bends willingly. Small adjustments, one at a time, the same discipline from the very first prompting lessons.
