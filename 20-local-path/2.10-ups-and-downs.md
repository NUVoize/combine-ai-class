# Lesson 2.10: The Ups and the Downs, Honestly

You can train a LoRA now. This lesson steps back and tells you the truth about them, the good and the bad, before you build your whole workflow around one. This is the "no magic button" promise of the course applied to the most powerful tool in it. LoRAs are not a perfect, do-everything answer, and knowing their real edges is what keeps you from frustration later.

## What LoRAs are genuinely great at

- **Locked identity.** This is the headline. A well-trained LoRA holds your character across scenes, poses, and styles in a way nothing in the cloud path could. The consistency problem from Lesson 0.5 is, finally, solved.
- **Repeatability.** Once it is trained, you do not refight the battle each image. The character comes out right, again and again, with no per-shot fiddling.
- **Freedom.** Your character, your model, your rules. Generate as much as you want, however you want.
- **Shareable and stackable.** A LoRA is a small file. You can keep a whole cast of characters and reach for any of them.

## Where LoRAs let you down

Being honest, because you will meet all of these:

- **They are only as good as the dataset.** Everything from Module 2B comes back here. A weak dataset makes a weak LoRA, and no setting rescues it.
- **The under/over-training tightrope is real** (Lesson 2.9). It takes attempts to feel it, and your first ones may miss.
- **They change the base model a little** (Lesson 2.11, next). A LoRA does not sit perfectly apart from the model. It reshapes it, and that has side effects.
- **They can fight back when pushed** (Lesson 2.12). Train too hard or stack carelessly and the model resists you in ways that can be hard to diagnose.
- **They are narrow.** A character LoRA does one job. It is not a general upgrade, and treating it like one causes problems.

> **[FILL LATER: your honest war stories]** A few real examples from your own training history, the close to a thousand LoRAs: a LoRA that came out great, one that fought you, and what the difference turned out to be. This is the most credible content in the level. Nobody else can write it.

## The mindset that makes them work

Treat a LoRA as a sharp, specialized tool, not a wand. Respect the dataset, respect the training tightrope, and understand the side effects in the next two lessons. Do that and LoRAs become the most reliable thing in your kit. Ignore it and they become a source of mysterious, maddening problems.
