# Lesson 2.1: Why Go Local

> **Recap for jump-in buyers.** If you came straight to Level 2, here is the one paragraph you need from the Cloud Path. There, you learned to hold a character by steering the model from the outside: hand it a reference image, generate from the closest match, and repair the slips. It works, but nothing is ever truly locked, and you are always renting someone else's model and living inside their limits. This level is about removing that ceiling. You do not need Level 1 to follow along, but everything here is the answer to a problem Level 1 defines in full.

The Cloud Path ended at a wall (Lesson 1.9): you can steer a model from the outside, but you can never quite *lock* your character, and you are always a guest on someone else's platform. Going local is how you knock that wall down. Let's be clear-eyed about what you gain and what it costs.

## The three reasons to go local

**1. Control.** This is the big one. Local means you can reach *inside* the model, teach it your character until it carries the identity itself, and run any workflow you want. This is the real lock that the cloud path could only approximate.

**2. Freedom from limits.** No content filters, no queues, no per-image pricing, no rules that change overnight. The model is yours to run as often and however you like.

**3. Cost over time.** There is real upfront cost (hardware you buy or a GPU you rent), but no per-generation fee. Past a certain volume, local is simply cheaper, and if you generate a lot, that line gets crossed fast.

## The honest trade

Local is more powerful and more demanding. The setup is steeper, it asks real things of your hardware, and there is a learning curve the cloud path does not have. You are trading convenience for control. That is the deal, and for the work that needs locked, repeatable, unlimited character consistency, it is worth it.

## What this level delivers

By the end of the Local Path you will be able to build proper training datasets, train and deploy your own LoRAs, run local pipelines, and, just as important, have the judgment to know which tool fits which job. That judgment is the part most people never develop, and it is woven through every lesson here.
