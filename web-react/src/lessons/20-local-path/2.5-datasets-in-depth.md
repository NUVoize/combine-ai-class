# Lesson 2.5: Training Datasets in Depth

In the Cloud Path, you met the term **training dataset** in its quick form: a small, curated folder of images of your character that you pulled references from (Lesson 1.7). Same name, much bigger job now. In the local world, a training dataset is not a folder you pick from. It is the actual material you use to *teach* a model who your character is. The dataset is the single biggest factor in whether your training succeeds. Better than any setting, better than any model choice: get the dataset right and everything downstream gets easier.

So this module is not a footnote. It is the heart of local work.

## Why the dataset is everything

When you train, the model learns from exactly what you show it, and *only* from what you show it. It cannot learn an angle you never gave it. It will happily learn a flaw you did not notice. The dataset is the curriculum, and the model is a literal student. Everything good and everything broken in your final result traces back to here.

This is the lesson that two years and close to a thousand LoRAs teaches loudest: people blame their settings when the real problem was almost always the dataset.

## What actually makes a dataset good

A strong training dataset is not just "a lot of pictures." It is a deliberate spread:

- **Consistency of identity.** Every image must be unmistakably the same character. One off-model image teaches the model the wrong face.
- **Variety of everything else.** Different angles, expressions, lighting, distances, and settings, *while the identity stays fixed*. Variety in the surroundings is what lets the model separate "the character" from "the background."
- **Quality.** Clean, clear images. Blurry, low-resolution, or heavily compressed images teach blur and artifacts.
- **The right size.** Enough to cover the character, not so many that you drown it in noise or redundancy. More is not automatically better.

> **[FILL LATER: good vs bad dataset]** Side by side: a clean, well-spread dataset and a sloppy one (inconsistent identity, all same angle, low quality), with the trained result each one produces. This single comparison sells the whole module.

> **[FILL LATER: your dataset rules of thumb]** Your real numbers and instincts: rough image counts, how much variety, the spread you aim for. The hard-won stuff.

## The mental model to carry forward

Think of it as showing the model the same person across many different days, never the same day twice. Same face, always. Everything else, always changing. That contrast is exactly what teaches the model to hold the identity while letting the scene move, which is the whole goal of this course, finally solved from the inside.
