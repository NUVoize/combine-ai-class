# Lesson 2.4: RunPods and Rented GPUs

Lesson 2.3 may have felt discouraging if your machine is modest. This lesson is the fix. You can rent a powerful GPU by the hour, train on hardware far beyond anything you own, and shut it down when you are done. This single option removes hardware as a barrier to entry, and it is how a lot of serious local work actually gets done, even by people who own good cards.

## The idea

Instead of buying a GPU, you rent time on someone else's. You spin up a machine in the cloud with the exact card you need, do your training or generation, and pay only for the hours you used. When you stop, the cost stops. A heavy training run that would need a card you cannot afford becomes a few dollars of rental.

This is different from the cloud *tools* of Level 1. There you rented someone's model and lived inside their rules. Here you rent a raw machine and run *your own* local setup on it, with full control. It is local work, just on borrowed hardware.

## When renting beats buying

- You want to train on a heavy model family your card cannot handle.
- You only train occasionally, so owning an expensive card would sit idle.
- You want to experiment with several model families before committing to hardware.
- You need to train fast and your own card is slow.

And when buying wins: you train constantly, every day, where rental hours would add up past the price of a card. It is the same cost-over-time logic from Lesson 2.1, applied to your own habits.

## The mindset that saves you money

The meter is running, so work clean. Have your dataset prepared *before* you start the machine, know your settings going in, and shut it down the moment you are done. Most wasted rental cost is a machine left running while someone reads documentation. Prepare on your own time, rent only to compute.
