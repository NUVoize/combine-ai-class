# Lesson 2.9: Training Your First LoRA

Everything so far has been preparation. Now you train. This lesson is the hands-on walkthrough: take your prepared, captioned dataset and turn it into a working LoRA of your character. We will keep the concepts here and leave the exact settings as fill-in, because the right numbers depend on your model family and your training tool, and those are exactly where your experience should lead.

## Before you start

You should already have, from this module: a clean dataset (2.5), prepared for your model family (2.6), captioned and balanced (2.7). Do not start training to "see what happens" with a weak dataset. You will only learn that the dataset was weak, slowly and expensively. Training is the reward for good preparation, not a substitute for it.

> **[FILL LATER: tool setup]** Your chosen training tool, installed and configured, screen-recorded. Where students get it, how to point it at their dataset, and the layout you use.

## The training loop, conceptually

Without drowning in settings, here is what happens when you train:

1. The tool shows the model your images and captions, over and over, in rounds.
2. Each round, the model nudges the LoRA slightly so it gets better at reproducing your character.
3. It saves the LoRA at intervals, so you end with several versions from different points in training.
4. You test those versions and keep the best one.

That last point matters more than beginners expect: training does not produce one answer, it produces a *range*, and picking the right point along it is a skill.

## Undertrained vs overtrained

This is the core judgment of training, so learn to see it:

- **Undertrained:** the LoRA barely learned your character. Identity is weak and drifts. You did not train enough, or the dataset was too thin.
- **Overtrained:** the LoRA learned too hard. It reproduces your character rigidly, often dragging in memorized poses and backgrounds, and it fights any new scene. It can also look fried or distorted.
- **The sweet spot:** strong identity, still flexible. This is what you are hunting for among your saved versions.
